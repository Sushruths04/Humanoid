Simulation Setup

How we actually run the training — thousands of robots at once, and the trick that makes it transfer to the real world.

Thousands of robots, one GPU

Isaac Sim renders 8,192 copies of the G1 in parallel on a single GPU, each trying slightly different things. At ~100,000+ simulation steps per second, the robot accumulates years of practice in hours. This raw speed is the whole method — it's why a simple algorithm like PPO is enough; we don't need a clever sample-efficient one.

What the robot sees and does

👁️

Observations (input)

Joint angles & speeds, body orientation, base velocity, the previous action, and task info (board angle, rope force, a phase clock, the command embedding).

🦾

Actions (output)

Target positions for every motor, sent to a PD controller. The policy outputs these ~50 times a second.

Domain Randomization — the sim-to-real trick

A policy trained in one perfect simulation overfits it and fails on a real robot, where friction, weight, and motor strength are never exactly known. The fix: randomize those unknowns every episode. The robot can't memorize one setup, so it learns a behavior that works across a whole range — which then covers reality.

We randomize…RangeWhy
Terrainflat → procedural rough groundreal ground is uneven
Joint stiffness & damping±25%real motors aren't ideal
Link masses±10%payload/build varies
Friction (wakeboard "sand")μ 0.25–0.6surface varies
Rope pull (wakeboard)±15% + direction jitterthe boat tug is never identical
It worked. With ±25% joint randomization on rough terrain, our G1 stays upright 981 / 1000 steps — strong evidence the policy generalized rather than memorized.

The training loop in code-shape

# conceptually, every iteration:
obs        = env.observe()              # 8192 robots' states
actions    = policy(obs)                # the neural-net "brain"
next_obs, reward, done = env.step(actions)
policy     = PPO.update(obs, actions, reward)  # nudge toward more reward
Interview answer — "how did you set up the sim / handle sim-to-real?"
"Isaac Lab with 8k parallel G1 envs at ~100k steps/s on an L40S; PD position-control actions, proprioceptive + task observations, PPO via RSL-RL. For sim-to-real I rely on extensive domain randomization — terrain, ±25% joint stiffness/damping, link mass, friction, and push/pull disturbances — so the policy is robust to the reality gap. My robustness run reaching 981/1000 episode length under randomization is the evidence it transfers."

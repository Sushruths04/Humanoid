CHAPTER 09

Why These Choices

The questions interviewers love: why this model/method and not the trendy alternatives? Here are honest, defensible answers.

Why NVIDIA GR00T (for the foundation-model track)?

  • It's a humanoid foundation model. GR00T (N1.x, ~3B params) is pre-trained on broad humanoid motion/manipulation data and designed to be fine-tuned to a specific robot — exactly our use case.
  • It fits the ecosystem. It's NVIDIA's own model, aligned with Isaac Sim/Lab, the G1, and the GPUs we already use.
  • It's accessible. Open weights on Hugging Face, a documented fine-tune + eval path — so a solo project can actually run it.

Why NOT the popular alternatives?

AlternativeWhat it isWhy not here
π0 (Physical Intelligence)A flow-matching VLA for dexterous manipulationBuilt for tabletop arm/hand manipulation from teleop data — not whole-body legged locomotion, and not a sim-RL workflow. Wrong shape for "make a humanoid stand/walk".
OpenVLAA 7B VLA for single-arm manipulation (trained on Open X-Embodiment)It outputs 7-DoF arm actions from real-robot demos. We need 20+ DoF whole-body control learned in sim — different problem, no demo dataset.
World models (Dreamer / TD-MPC)Learn a model of the world for sample-efficient RLTheir whole advantage is needing fewer samples. We have a blisteringly fast simulator (100k+ steps/s), so samples are basically free — a world model adds complexity and instability for no payoff. Model-free PPO is simpler and is the proven standard for humanoid locomotion.
End-to-end imitation onlyCopy human/teleop demosNo large wakeboard/locomotion demo set exists, and the physics of balance & the rope must be learned, not copied. We use imitation (AMP) only for style, not as the main objective.

Why PPO (and not something fancier)?

Because simulation is cheap and fast, sample efficiency doesn't matter — so the winning move is the most stable, well-understood algorithm, not the most sample-efficient one. PPO's clipped update is robust to the noisy, contact-rich rewards of locomotion, and RSL-RL runs it at huge scale on GPU. It's what essentially every state-of-the-art humanoid locomotion result uses.

Why Isaac Lab (and not MuJoCo / a custom sim)?

  • Parallelism: thousands of GPU envs out of the box — the throughput that makes PPO practical.
  • Batteries included: a ready G1 model, manager-based rewards/observations, domain-randomization tools, and a clean RSL-RL bridge.
  • Sim-to-real track record: the recent humanoid locomotion papers (HumanUP, Booster Gym, Humanoid-Gym) are built on this exact lineage.
Interview answer — "why GR00T and not π0 / OpenVLA / a world model?"
"Those are excellent but solve a different problem: π0 and OpenVLA are manipulation VLAs trained on real-robot demos, not whole-body legged control in sim. World models buy sample efficiency, which I don't need because Isaac Lab gives me 100k+ steps/s — so model-free PPO is simpler and the proven standard. GR00T is the natural fit for the foundation-model track because it's a humanoid model designed for fine-tuning and it's open and integrated with the NVIDIA stack I'm already on. I use imitation (AMP) only for motion style, not as the primary objective."
